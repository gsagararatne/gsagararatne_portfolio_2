Rating FRIENDS with NLP

31/8/2021 3-minute read

Predicted IMDb show ratings of FRIENDS based on the number of times a character spoke.

Video Summary


Backstory

I refuse to to watch the last few episodes or last season of a TV show because I don’t want the TV show to end. That got me thinking of ways to predict show ratings. No, I still haven’t finished the last season of FREINDS… but if I do go back to it… was I on a break?

Project Summary

  • Analysed and performed NLP on script datasets of FRIENDS from kaggle to identify how each FRIENDS character affected the IMDb ratings
  • Created a pipeline and developed a Logistic Regression model to predict 218 episode show ratings of FRIENDS with a cross validation score of 61.4%
  • Implemented XGBoost to achieve a maximum 5 fold cv score of of 62.6%
  • Implemented Deep Learning (TensorFlow) model to achieve 5-fold cv score of 55.45%
  • Further quantified validation scores by analysing precision, recall, AUC and ROC scores of machine learning models

Project Results

Feature Engineering

thumbs_up is a feature that classifies ratings above the mean rating as 1, and 0 when below. The mean rating for the show is 8.4/10 from an IMDb score rate Observations:

  • Episodes written by more than one writer seemed to score higher ratings
  • Episodes directed by Ross (David Schwimmer) and Kevin Bright seemed to score higher ratings
  • Episodes written by Gary Halvorson seemed to be tied
  • Episodes written by other directors seemed to score lower than the average ratings

Final Model

Modelling Procedure:

  • I ran a Grid Search on 3 main models to identify my base model
    • Decision Tree
    • Logistic Regression
    • KNN
  • With Logistic Regression as the best base model, I fine tuned it with another Grid Search
  • Model stats
    • Max 5 fold cross validation score of 61.4%
    • ROC: 0.52
    • Type I (False Pos) error: 25%
    • Type II (False Neg) error: 22.7%
    • Precision (Pos Predictive Value): 54%
    • Recall (True Pos Value): 57%

Personally am not satisfied with how my modelling turned out, and I strongly believe it’s due to the lack of data available. I intend to do another project with a lot more data available.

Main Features

Main features I modelled with:

  • IMDB stats
  • Writers and directors
  • Character lines

With us_viewers_mill being the most prominent positive feature, a little EDA to showcase the behaviour throughout the 10 seasons of FRIENDS. A dampened Sine wave pattern can be observed from Season 1-10.

Character Lines

Most positive and negative character line features:

  • Positive
    • Joey
      • For every additional line that Joey talks, the odds of a thumbs up increases by 15.2%
    • Janice
      • For every additional line that Janice talks, the odds of a thumbs up increases by 5.8%
  • Negative
    • Chandler
      • For every additional line that Chandler talks, the odds of a thumbs up decreases by 12.5%
    • Rachel
      • For every additional line that Rachel talks, the odds of a thumbs up decreases by 8.7%

XG Boost and Deep Learning

  • XGBoost: Max 5 fold CV score of 62.6%
  • Deep Learning: Max 5 fold CV score of 55.45%
