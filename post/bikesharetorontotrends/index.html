Bike Share Toronto Trends

4/1/2022 2-minute read

Analysed bike usage from Bike Share Toronto to bring in usage insights of 5 years and forecast usage for 2 years using time-series.

Video Summary

COMING SOON

Backstory

I started biking to the gym with my roommate during the summer of 2021. While he had his own bike, I would rent a bike for every use from Bike Share Toronto. We’d ride to the gym during sunny and rainy days. That got me thinking of this service’s usage.

Project Summary

  • Analysed 5 years worth of data and drove insightful visuals of member and non member usage trends through an interactive Tableau dashboard
  • Conducted Rolling Mean, ADF and KPSS tests to confirm stationarity of data
  • Forecasted 2 years of bike usage by comparing the MAE scores of SARIMA and F-Prophet time-series models

Project Results

Stationary tests

  • The Rolling Mean test visually showed that the mean and variance were not constant due to the upward trend.
  • The Augmented Dickey-Fuller test (ADF) test on base data proved that the data was not stationary. However, the ADF test on the log of the base data proved to be stationary.
  • The Kwiatkowski-Phillips-Schmidt-Shin (KPSS) test on base data proved that the series is stationary around the deterministic trend.

Building SARIMA model

After plotting the Auto Correlation of the 1st difference, 1 and 7 were valid values for the p (AR) terms. This also secured the d=1 (I) term.

After plotting the Partial Auto Correlation of the the 1st difference, 1 seemed to be a vlid value for the q (MA) term.

Compairing SARIMA and F-Prophet

Ran a small loop to attain more accurate MAE and RMSE scores for SARIMA. However, F-Prophet seemed to be the most accurate model.
